## Mobile truncation rule

LinkedIn cuts the post at **~210 characters** on mobile before the "…see more" button. **The first 2 lines have to earn the click.** Every variant below is built so the first ~210 chars are a complete, intriguing thought.

## My recommendation: **Variant A**

Reasons:
1. It opens with a concrete announcement ("opening my freelance practice") — no ambiguity about what the post is for.
2. The "2-day scramble → 1-week estimate → 4 days actual" anecdote is specific and re-tellable.
3. Real numbers and real company names (with your permission) buy more credibility than abstract claims.
4. The "3 clients this quarter" scarcity is honest and not manufactured.
5. "No pitch, no slides" disarms the reader's hustle-post defenses.
