## Mobile truncation rule

LinkedIn cuts the post at **~210 characters** on mobile before the "…see more" button. **The first 2 lines have to earn the click.** Every variant below is built so the first ~210 chars are a complete, intriguing thought.

## My recommendation: **Variant A**

Reasons:
1. The opening line does two things LinkedIn's algorithm loves: announces something new AND validates credentials — "some of India's best EdTechs" + "both online bootcamps and offline coaching institutes" is high-signal positioning without naming names.
2. Readers from EdTech will self-identify with either the online or offline model.
3. The "2-day scramble → 1-week estimate → 4 days actual" anecdote is specific and re-tellable (no company name, still believable).
4. Real numbers (85K+, 60K+, 80%, 20hrs/week) buy more credibility than abstract claims.
5. The "3 clients this quarter" scarcity is honest and not manufactured.
6. "No pitch, no slides" disarms the reader's hustle-post defenses.
